# PCS Vector Strategic Plan

## 1. Executive Summary & Recommended Strategy
**Primary recommendation: Prioritize off-post rental in Hope Mills (28348) or Spring Lake (28390) within the $1,550–$1,700 range.** This location delivers the strongest combination of BAH surplus, 15–25 minute commutes, and access to Cumberland County Schools while positioning your spouse for fastest district-level hiring and MyCAA-supported licensure. It directly supports the primary priority of spouse career momentum before the school year starts.

Ranked alternatives:  
1. Raeford (28376) for quieter fenced-yard options and slightly lower rents if school zoning confirms Harnett County acceptance.  
2. On-post housing only as a 30-day bridge if off-post inventory collapses.

Key reasoning: Spouse employment and school quality are interdependent here; off-post gives you leverage on both while preserving $200–$300 monthly flexibility. Main risk/dependency is NC teaching license processing (4–8 weeks) colliding with fixed reporting date.  

**If licensure or childcare slots slip past 45 days, fall back to substitute teaching in Cumberland County plus ACS emergency FCC placement.** Blind-spot insight: Summer humidity will push off-post electric bills to the high end of the $150–$250 range exactly when you are trying to stabilize cash flow for the new school year.

## 2. Spouse Career & Childcare Plan
Lead with NC licensure reciprocity immediately upon orders. Submit the packet the day it drops—expect 4–8 weeks for full approval. Use the substitute-teaching fast path through Cumberland County Schools as a bridge to first paycheck within 10–14 days of application.  

**Fast-track options:** Apply to Cape Fear Valley Health education-adjacent roles and Skibo Road retail/district support positions for interim income while waiting on the license. Leverage **MSEP** for direct district connections and **MyCAA** to cover any reciprocity fees or testing. Attend ACS spouse employment workshops at the Soldier Support Center within the first week on station.  

Childcare bottleneck is real: CDC waitlists run 30–90 days for school-age; submit DD Form 2606 same day orders are received. Shift to Family Child Care homes in Hope Mills/Spring Lake for 2–4 week openings while waiting. This sequence protects your spouse’s momentum and keeps both children covered without derailing the teaching timeline.

## 3. Housing Strategy & Cost Tradeoffs
**I would lock an off-post 3BR rental at or below $1,700** to stay inside your $1,800 ceiling while capturing BAH surplus for fenced-yard upgrades or pet deposits. On-post is only viable as a short-term safety net due to variable waitlists.

## 4. Financial Opportunities & DITY/PPM Considerations
With 1,180 miles and two driving days, run the numbers on a **partial DITY (HHG only)**. Estimated government payout minus truck/fuel/packing typically nets $1,200–$3,000. Full DITY can reach $1,500–$4,000 if you maximize weight allowance, but adds risk with pets and two school-age children in the vehicle.  

I recommend partial DITY: it preserves cash flow without overextending family capacity. Use TLE at the gaining installation for up to 10 days while securing the off-post lease. Time the move so TLE bridges any gap between arrival and first spouse paycheck.

## 5. Getting Settled Fast – First 30 Days Action Plan

**Days 16–30 (Employment stabilization gate):** Begin substitute or district support work; confirm MyCAA reimbursement. If spouse has not started paid work by day 25, activate ACS emergency placement and remote teaching options.
